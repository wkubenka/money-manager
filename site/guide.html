Installation

Download Latest Release

macOS

  1. Download the .dmg file from the latest release.
  2. Open the .dmg and drag Astute Money to your Applications folder.
  3. On first launch, macOS may show “Astute Money can’t be opened because it is from an unidentified developer.”
    To fix this: open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click “Open Anyway”.
  4. The app will launch and create a local database automatically.

Windows

  1. Download the .exe installer from the latest release.
  2. Run the installer. Windows SmartScreen may show a warning — click “More info” then “Run anyway”.
  3. Follow the installer prompts. Astute Money will be added to your Start menu.
  4. Launch the app — it will create a local database automatically.

Conscious Spending Plans

A conscious spending plan divides your take-home pay into four categories, following Ramit Sethi’s recommended ranges:

Fixed Costs
50–60%
Investments
10%
Savings
5–10%
Guilt-Free
20–35%

Getting started

  1. Navigate to Spending Plans in the sidebar and click Create New Plan.
  2. Enter your monthly take-home income (and optionally your gross income and pre-tax investment contributions like 401k).
  3. Add line items to Fixed Costs, Investments, and Savings. Guilt-Free is calculated automatically as whatever’s left over.
  4. Color-coded badges show whether each category is within the ideal range.
  5. Mark a plan as Current to have it appear on your dashboard and track spending against it.

Tips

  • Use the Miscellaneous % field on Fixed Costs for irregular expenses you can’t itemize.
  • Create multiple plans to compare scenarios (e.g., “After Raise” or “If I Move Cities”).
  • Copy an existing plan to use it as a starting point.

Net Worth Tracking

Track your complete financial picture across four account categories: Assets (home, car), Investments (401k, brokerage), Savings (emergency fund, vacation), and Debt (credit cards, loans).

Net Worth = Assets + Investments + Savings − Debt.

Adding accounts

  1. Go to Net Worth in the sidebar.
  2. Click the + button next to the category you want.
  3. Enter the account name and current balance.
  4. For debt accounts, you can also enter the interest rate and minimum payment — these are used by the Debt Payoff calculator.

Emergency fund

Mark one savings account as your emergency fund. The dashboard will show how many months of expenses it covers based on your current spending plan.

Expense Tracking

Track what you actually spend and compare it against your conscious spending plan.

Expense accounts

Create an account for each source of spending (e.g., “Chase Sapphire”, “Checking Account”). Each account has its own tab on the Expenses page.

Adding expenses

  1. Go to Expenses and select the account tab.
  2. Click Add Expense and enter the merchant, amount, date, and category.
  3. Once you categorize a merchant, the app remembers it — next time you enter that merchant, the category is filled in automatically.

CSV import

Import bank or credit card statements as CSV files. The importer automatically detects columns, prevents duplicates, and lets you categorize in bulk by merchant.

  1. Download a CSV from your bank or credit card provider.
  2. Click Import CSV on the expense account tab.
  3. Map the columns (date, merchant, amount) and import.
  4. Use the Uncategorized tab to quickly categorize imported transactions.

Dashboard tracking

When you have a current spending plan, the dashboard shows progress bars for each category — how much you’ve spent vs. how much you planned, with the remaining amount highlighted.

Debt Payoff

Model different strategies to pay off your debts faster. Requires debt accounts with interest rates and minimum payments set on the Net Worth page, and a “Debt Payments” line item in your spending plan’s Fixed Costs.

Strategies

Avalanche

Pay minimums on everything, then throw extra money at the highest interest rate debt first. Saves the most money overall.

Snowball

Pay minimums on everything, then throw extra money at the smallest balance first. Gives faster psychological wins.

Scenarios

Add scenarios to compare strategies side by side. Try adding extra monthly payments or a one-time lump sum to see how it changes your payoff date and total interest paid. Charts show the balance over time, payoff order, and interest comparison.

Rich Life Vision

Define what a “rich life” means to you. This is the qualitative side of personal finance — not just numbers, but what you want your money to make possible.

  • Add vision items to your dashboard (“Travel to Japan every year”, “Never stress about a restaurant bill”).
  • Organize them into categories and drag to reorder.
  • Use the lock icon to toggle between viewing and editing mode.

Your Data

All data is stored locally in a SQLite database on your computer. Nothing is sent to any server.

Export & import

Go to Settings → Data to export your data as a JSON file or import a previously exported backup. This is useful for migrating to a new computer.

Updating

Download the latest release from GitHub and install it over your existing installation. Your data will be preserved.
